# Standing Out as a Candidate in Today's Job Market
*Key strategies extracted from Vaco by Highspring's 2026 Talent Pulse.*

When the Resume Expert Agent formats or creates a resume, or prepares a candidate for an interview, the following 5 critical areas must be incorporated:

## 1. Strategies to Stand Out as a Candidate
Standing out requires a strategic, proactive approach to applications:
- **Highlight Measurable Achievements:** Portfolios and resumes must highlight unique skills and *quantifiable* achievements.
- **Tailor Every Application:** The resume and cover letter must specifically align with the exact requirements of the target role.
- **Build the Brand:** Engage in ongoing industry conversations (networking) and continuously upskill.

## 2. Ways to Prepare for a First Interview
A polished resume lands the interview, but preparation wins it:
- **Research:** Check the website, LinkedIn, Glassdoor, and network for insights on goals, culture, and values.
- **Review:** Connect past experience directly to the job description's specific needs.
- **Scenarios (STAR Method):** Gather specific examples of challenges overcome and measurable achievements that match the job description.

## 3. How AI Talent is Shaping Organizations
Organizations are rethinking how capability is built across their workforce:
- Companies are prioritizing candidates who can safely deploy and scale transformative technologies.
- Candidates must demonstrate a focus on agility, resilience, and protecting institutional knowledge alongside AI adoption.

## 4. Steps to Build an Effective AI Talent Strategy
When positioning a candidate for senior or AI-adjacent roles, emphasize these traits:
- **Teach, Don't Just Execute:** Highlight the ability to mentor and transfer knowledge, embedding expertise into the organization.
- **Continuous Learning:** Show a history of cultivating curiosity, attending external sessions, and sharing insights internally.
- **Process Documentation:** Show a track record of documenting processes and fostering systems that survive employee turnover.

## 5. Insights from the Field (The Talent Pulse)
Understanding the realities of the 2026 job market helps set candidate expectations:
- **Slower Hiring:** 47% of candidates face too many interviews, and 46% face slow feedback. Persistence is mandatory.
- **Networking is Mandatory:** 55% rely on referrals or networking, though it takes time to yield results. 
- **Longer Searches:** Over 53% of searches take 6+ months.
- **Cultural Fit Matters:** 31% of people leave jobs within 6 months if the alignment/culture isn't right. Resumes must screen for culture fit as much as technical skills.
